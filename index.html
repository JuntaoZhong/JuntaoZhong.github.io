About Me:

I'm an international student at Carleton College (Class of 2023), Minnesota. I major in Biology and Computer Science, and I want to pursuit a career in Bioinformatics. I am most interested in Precision Medicine (building phylogenetic tree for cancer cells, and finding treatments for it), and the human gut microbiome (the interaction and population dynamic of all the bacteria that live inside us)!

At Professor Rika Anderson’s lab, I currently investigate the cause of the high abundance of transposase (a virus-like jumping gene) in deep oceans. I script software pipelines to analyze the Tara Oceans dataset (Terabytes of DNA and RNA sequencing data) from the world’s oceans. I focus on the evolution of microbes recovered from metagenomic assembled genomes (MAGs).

For summer 2021, I intern at Hewlett Packard Enterprise. I work on the operating system of their supercomputers. I still have a lot of "room for improvment" in my Shell Scripts and C++ skills.

For summer 2020, I did psychology experiments at Dr. Julie Neiworth's Primate Cognition Lab to investigate how cognitive failures of aging cotton-top tamarins (Saguinus oedipus) relate to biology events in their brains. I enjoy washing monkey cages, and feeding monkeys with food better than mine.
